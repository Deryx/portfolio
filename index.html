about me

My name is pronounced Dair · ricks, not Dexter like a 3rd grade substitute teacher once called me (still smh!). I am a Web Developer/Database Administrator at a small liberal arts college in Westchester County, NY.

While attending high school in Freeport, Long Island, I became enamored with computers when I saw the Commodore 64 my senior year. I immediately joined the computer club and had a miserable time because I had no idea about programming!

In the late-nineties after again flailing at programming while attending Stanford, finally getting programming at Stanford and getting a MPA from New York University, I was transfixed by the Internet. Some friends and I got together to create an online magazine. I learned HTML in a week. Never did any programming on the site (it was never created), but I did create an interactive personal training website within two weeks of learning HTML (and Perl!). A year later, after creating a financial aid estimator program in HTML and classic ASP for my aforementioned employer, I transitioned to the job I now have.

Why web development? It primarily satisfies the two sides of my persona — the problem-solving, analytical side and the artistic, creative side. I love hand-coding functionally interactive websites that are professional, symmetrical and attractive. Web development also demands that I continually learn new technologies.

case study: my spanish tutor

specs

company:
personal
technology:
HTML5, CSS3, AngularJS, jQuery, jQueryUI, JavaScript, Animate.css, Bootstrap, JSON
website:
http://deryx.github.io/spanish-tutor

My Spanish Tutor came about because I wanted to further learn how to program in AngularJS. This was my second foray into Angular programming.

I had been teaching myself Spanish for a while and I wanted a somewhat fun vehicle to continue my Spanish/Angular learning. So, I created a suite of tools to help with learning Spanish:

  • flashcards to learn vocabulary and verb conjugation;
  • vocabulary slider game where you slide spanish word tiles to their corresponding images and english definitions;
  • a word scramble game where a jumbled spanish word is presented and it is your job to correctly unscramble the word;
  • a word completion game where you are presented with incomplete spanish words and it is your job to fill in the blanks; and
  • a verb conjugator game where you are presented with with a spanish verb and you have to conjugate the verb in the present, preterite, imperfect or future tenses (you choose).

At the moment, this is very basic, beginning Spanish. However, more functionality and difficulty will be added in the future as I learn more Spanish.

case study: net price calculator

specs

company:
personal
technology:
HTML5, CSS3, AngularJS, jQuery, jQueryUI, JSON
website:
http://deryx.github.io/npc

After doing some fairly rudimentary programming in AngularJS, I decided it was time to create something a bit larger in scope. I had already created the original Net Price Calculator for my employer using ASP.NET MVC (C#), HTML, CSS, jQuery, Bootstrap and XML. Since I was not saving or working with any sensitive data, I did not need a server to store or retrieve data. Therefore, this was a perfect project to use AngularJS since everything is done through the client.

The application is essentially a wizard program, taking the user through 5 screens, if the student is independent, or 6 screens, if the student is dependent. The final screen is always the results. The application calculates a student's estimated family contribution (efc) using the Federal Methodology, the same methodology used when a student completes the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA). On the results page, the student is presented with the College of New Rochelle's cost of attendance, total amount of state grants and scholarships, total amount of college grants and scholarships and the net amount the student is responsible for after all estimated aid is subtracted from the cost of attendance.
