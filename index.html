Introduction

about me

My name is pronounced Dair · ricks, not Dexter like a 3rd grade substitute teacher once called me (still smh!). I am a Web Developer/Database Administrator at a small liberal arts college in Westchester County, NY.

While attending high school in Freeport, Long Island, I became enamored with computers when I saw the Commodore 64 my senior year. I immediately joined the computer club and had a miserable time because I had no idea about programming!

In the late-nineties after again flailing at programming while attending Stanford, finally getting programming at Stanford and getting a MPA from New York University, I was transfixed by the Internet. Some friends and I got together to create an online magazine. I learned HTML in a week. Never did any programming on the site (it was never created), but I did create an interactive personal training website within two weeks of learning HTML (and Perl!). A year later, after creating a financial aid estimator program in HTML and classic ASP for my aforementioned employer, I transitioned to the job I now have.

Why web development? It primarily satisfies the two sides of my persona — the problem-solving, analytical side and the artistic, creative side. I love hand-coding functionally interactive websites that are professional, symmetrical and attractive. Web development also demands that I continually learn new technologies.
